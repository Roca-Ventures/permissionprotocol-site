The Problem

AI systems can now deploy code, move money, mutate data, and call external APIs. But there is no standard way to answer one critical question: who authorized this AI action — and under what policy?

Silent autonomy

Actions execute without explicit authority or accountable approvals.

Retroactive logging

Logs after the fact don’t prevent damage or satisfy auditors.

Undefined liability

When AI acts, responsibility becomes ambiguous — legally and operationally.

What Permission Protocol Defines

Permission Protocol is a control-plane standard for autonomous systems. It defines how AI requests permission, how policies are enforced, and how actions are approved and audited.

Execution Requests

Agents submit structured requests describing intent and inputs. Direct execution is blocked.

Policy Supremacy

Policies override intelligence. Scope, spend, time windows, and environment locks are enforced.

Receipts & Audit

Every action yields a verifiable permission receipt for incident response and compliance.

Reference Implementation

Access to the Permission Protocol reference implementation is granted selectively. Requests are reviewed to ensure alignment with operational scope, risk profile, and intended use.
