The Problem

AI systems can now deploy code, move money, mutate data, and call external APIs. But there is no standard way to answer one critical question: who authorized this AI action — and under what policy?

Silent Autonomy

Most AI systems execute actions without explicit authority or human accountability.

Retroactive Logging

Logs after the fact don’t prevent damage or satisfy regulators.

Undefined Liability

When AI acts, responsibility is unclear — legally and operationally.

What Permission Protocol Defines

Permission Protocol is a control-plane standard for autonomous systems. It defines how AI requests permission, how policies are enforced, and how actions are approved and audited.

Execution Requests

AI cannot act directly. It must request permission.

Policy Supremacy

Policies override intelligence. Always.

Human Authority

Irreversible actions require named, recorded approval.

Immutable Receipts

Every decision produces a verifiable audit artifact.
